What happened

You clicked a phishing email.

Last week, the IT team ran a planned phishing simulation as part of our annual security programme. The email you received from it-support@enquiry.bot was the test. This training will show you exactly what the red flags were and how to spot them next time.

No real harm has been done. This was a safe, controlled exercise. No real credentials were captured and your accounts are secure. This training is here to help — not to judge.

What to do next time

How to handle suspicious emails.

Phishing emails are designed by professionals to look convincing. The key is to slow down and check before you click — not after.

1
Check the sender domainClick on the sender name to reveal the full email address. Our real domains are ebtechgroup.com, boxly.ai, beami.ai and enquirybot.com. Anything else is suspicious.
2
Watch for urgency language"Expires today", "act immediately", "within 6 hours" — these are pressure tactics. Slow down whenever you see them.
3
Don't click — go directly to the siteIf you get an email about your Boxly account, open a new tab and go to boxly.ai directly. Never use a link in the email.
4
Report it to ITForward suspicious emails to the IT Service Desk. Don't just delete it — reporting helps protect everyone.
5
If you entered your credentials anywhere suspiciousChange your password immediately on the real site and report it to IT right away. Speed matters — early action prevents a real incident.
When in doubt, don't click. It takes 10 seconds to check a sender domain. It takes much longer to deal with a compromised account.
