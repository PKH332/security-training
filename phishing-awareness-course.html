What happened

You clicked a phishing email.

Last week, the IT team ran a planned phishing simulation as part of our annual security programme. The email you received from it-support@enquiry.bot was the test. This training will show you exactly what the red flags were and how to spot them next time.

No real harm has been done. This was a safe, controlled exercise. No real credentials were captured and your accounts are secure. This training is here to help — not to judge.

Here's what the phishing email looked like:

Reset My Password

IT Support
Boxly Ltd
1
Sender domain: enquiry.botOur real company domains are ebtechgroup.com, boxly.ai, beami.ai and enquirybot.com. enquiry.bot is not one of them. This alone should stop you from clicking.
2
Urgency tactics: "expires today", "immediately", "6 hours"Phishing emails create panic to stop you thinking clearly. Legitimate IT communications give reasonable notice — they don't demand action within hours.

The landing page

What you landed on after clicking.

After clicking the link, you were taken to a fake password reset page. Here's what it looked like and what to check whenever you land on a page asking for your credentials.

⚠️ college-lcd-defeat-timely.trycloudflare.com/?rid=umdu8oH

Reset Your Password

1
The URL is not a company domaincollege-lcd-defeat-timely.trycloudflare.com is not boxly.ai or any EB Tech domain. Always check the address bar before entering any credentials. If the URL looks unfamiliar, close the tab immediately.
2
Check the URL before entering anythingEven if the page looks familiar and asks for your current password, always verify the URL first. On a fake domain, anything you type goes straight to an attacker.
Rule: Check the address bar before you type anything. If the URL doesn't match the real company domain exactly, close the tab and report it to IT.

What to do next time

How to handle suspicious emails.

Phishing emails are designed by professionals to look convincing. The key is to slow down and check before you click — not after.

1
Check the sender domainClick on the sender name to reveal the full email address. Our real domains are ebtechgroup.com, boxly.ai and beami.ai. Anything else is suspicious.
2
Watch for urgency language"Expires today", "act immediately", "within 6 hours" — these are pressure tactics. Slow down whenever you see them.
3
Don't click — go directly to the siteIf you get an email about your Boxly account, open a new tab and go to boxly.ai directly. Never use a link in the email.
4
Report it to ITForward suspicious emails to the IT Service Desk. Don't just delete it — reporting helps protect everyone.
5
If you entered your credentials anywhere suspiciousChange your password immediately on the real site and report it to IT right away. Speed matters — early action prevents a real incident.
When in doubt, don't click. It takes 10 seconds to check a sender domain. It takes much longer to deal with a compromised account.
